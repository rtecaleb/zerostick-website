Overclocking

Increase your controller's polling rate for lower latency and more responsive inputs.

What is Overclocking?

Faster Inputs, Lower Latency

By default, the DualSense sends input data to your console or PC at 250Hz (every 4ms). Overclocking increases this to 1000Hz (every 1ms), meaning your button presses and stick movements are registered up to 4× faster.

This is especially noticeable in fast-paced games where reaction time matters — shooters, fighting games, and competitive titles.

How to Enable

Overclocking Guide

Note: Overclocking is only available when connected via USB. It is not active over Bluetooth. Make sure you're using a high-quality USB cable.
1

Connect via USB

Plug your ZeroStick into your PS5 or PC using the USB-C cable. Overclocking only works wired.

2

Hold the Overclock Button

Hold the dedicated overclock button (varies by build — refer to your build sheet) for 2 seconds. The light bar will turn blue to indicate overclocking is active.

3

Confirm in Your System

On PC, you can verify the polling rate using a free tool like HIDUSBF or Mouse Tester. On PS5, overclocking is active automatically when the light bar is blue.

4

To Disable

Hold the overclock button again for 2 seconds. The light bar will return to its standard color. The setting is not saved between power cycles — you must re-enable it each session.
